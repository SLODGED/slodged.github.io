Section Summary:

Draged to HELL was made in a game jam over the course three days. I really struggled with the prompt: Chain Reaction, and I wanted to do some really complex ideas but after one day I had nothing and took a new approach and took the prompt literally.

The theme of being the devil and dragging people into hell with a chain didn't come to me unti a few hours until submission.


I am quite proud of the end result and while I didn't win it was a great experiance and I learnt even more about creating games even faster.


All of the enemies share the same skeleton, meaning they can share animations without retargeting.

The chain is a cable which is a base component in Unreal Engine, I applied a masked chain material to it which gives it the gaps between the links.

The start of the cable is set to the players hand and when when an enemy is "grappled" the end of the cable is set to thier location constantly until the player lets go, the enemy will keep some of its velocity but will slow down quickly and ideally fall into a pit.

Base Enemy

This is the base enemy, this was the original colour for the player but when I changed the player to the devil I decided to switch the colours around. The AI is fairly basic and simply walks towards the player and punches them in attempt to knock them off the side.

The Big Guy

Here is the aptly named "Big Guy", he's my personal favorite. He moves slower but punches a lot harder, and if you are skilled enough you can swing him into the smaller enemies to kill them instantly.

Rock Thrower

Nearing the end of development, I decided that another enemy would help make the game more replayable. He simply throws rocks that lower the players health upon impact.
